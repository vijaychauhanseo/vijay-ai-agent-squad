## Core Concept
CompressedQualitySignals is described as the most important module in the entire Google Content Warehouse leak — it's the compressed quality scoring layer used in Mustang (page-level) and TeraGoogle (site-level) preliminary scoring that determines where pages enter the ranking pipeline.

## Key Takeaways
- CompressedQualitySignals feeds both Mustang (document) and TeraGoogle (site-level) preliminary scoring — it's the bedrock of ranking
- The Q* (Q-Star) quality framework is confirmed: it's a site-wide quality score based on authority, content quality, and user signals
- Google Panda lives on as a "content quality ghost" — site-level quality demotions from thin/low-quality content still apply
- NavBoost and CRAPS data flow directly into CompressedQualitySignals — behavioural demotions are real and persistent
- Six distinct categories of algorithmic penalties are stored and applied
- "People-first content" has granular evaluation criteria with specific sub-signals — not just a vague guideline
- Topicality, experimentation signals, and the future direction of ranking signals are all present in this structure
- The authority matrix (Q* framework) is the gateway — without baseline authority, other signals have less impact

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **Authority Matrix**: Site authority and Q* framework as preliminary scoring gate
- **Ghost of Panda**: Content quality as a site-wide demotion factor (inherited from Panda updates)
- **NavBoost/CRAPS Behavioural Demotions**: How click quality failures become persistent demotions
- **Algorithmic Penalty Taxonomy**: Six categories of automated penalties
- **People-First Content Evaluation**: Granular sub-signals for assessing whether content was made for people or search engines
- **Topicality Signals**: How topicality integrates into compressed quality scoring
- **Experimentation Signals**: How Google's ongoing ranking experiments are tracked in this structure
