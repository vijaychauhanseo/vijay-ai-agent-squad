## Core Concept
Google's leaked Content Warehouse reveals two quantitative systems for measuring topical authority: Site Radius (how wide your topic coverage extends) and Site Focus Score (how concentrated your content is on a core topic) — together they determine your site's T* (Topicality) score.

## Key Takeaways
- Google has moved from qualitative "authority" to quantitative topical relevance scores — this is now measurable
- Site Focus Score rewards sites that stay within a defined topic cluster — being too broad dilutes your score
- Site Radius measures how far your content strays from your core topic — smaller radius = stronger focus signal
- The leaked data shows two core ranking systems: Mustang (document-level) and TeraGoogle (site-level)
- Topical authority is built through depth + breadth within a niche — not by covering many unrelated topics
- The Google leak confirms "Site Quality" is a real score derived from topical coherence + E-E-A-T signals
- Internal linking architecture is critical — it signals to Google which pages are topically related
- Prioritised framework: E-E-A-T signals > content architecture > topical depth > AI-assisted scaling
