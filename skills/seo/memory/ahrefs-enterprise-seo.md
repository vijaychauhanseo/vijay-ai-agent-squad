## Core Concept
Enterprise SEO = SEO for large, complex websites or companies with multiple teams, technology stacks, and global operations. Even though @learnaiwithvijay is not enterprise-scale yet, this guide contains the most rigorous SEO frameworks available — applicable at any scale. Written by an ex-IBM in-house SEO.

## Key Takeaways
- Enterprise SEO differs from SMB SEO in scale, politics, and technology — not in core principles
- The biggest challenge: getting SEO changes implemented by dev/engineering teams (politics > tactics)
- Content at enterprise scale: programmatic SEO + content hubs + pillar pages
- Link building at enterprise scale: digital PR + partnerships + data studies
- Technical SEO at scale: site architecture, crawl budget, international hreflang, Core Web Vitals
- ROI calculation is essential — SEO must be justified in business terms to get budget

## Frameworks/Methods Covered

**Content Marketing:**
- Pillar pages + cluster content — comprehensive topic coverage to dominate a subject
- Programmatic SEO — templates for hundreds/thousands of similar pages (e.g., "best AI tools for [industry]")
- Content hubs — interlinked resource centers that build topical authority

**Link Building:**
- Data journalism — original research/studies that earn natural links
- Digital PR — newsworthy campaigns that get media coverage
- Partnerships — co-marketing with industry players

**Technical SEO:**
- Crawl budget management — ensure Googlebot indexes the right pages
- Site architecture — flat structure, logical URL hierarchy
- Core Web Vitals — LCP, FID, CLS directly affect rankings
- International: hreflang tags for multi-language/country sites

**Local + SaaS + Ecommerce specifics:**
- Local: NAP consistency, Google Business Profile optimization
- SaaS: focus on bottom-of-funnel keywords (tool comparisons, alternatives, pricing)
- Ecommerce: product page schema, faceted navigation management
