## Core Concept
SEO results typically take 3–6 months for established sites and 6–12+ months for new sites. The timeline depends on competition level, domain authority, content quality, and technical health. This article sets realistic expectations and shows how to accelerate results.

## Key Takeaways
- 3–6 months is the typical range to see meaningful organic traffic from new content
- New domains face the "Google sandbox" — extra delay before rankings stabilize
- High-competition keywords take longer; long-tail, low-competition terms can rank in weeks
- Factors that slow SEO down: weak domain authority, poor technical health, thin content, no backlinks
- Factors that speed it up: strong existing authority, good technical foundation, content depth, internal linking, active link building
- "Faster SEO" = target less competitive keywords first, build authority, then attack harder terms
- Ahrefs data: only 5.7% of newly published pages rank in top 10 within a year

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **Speed-up strategy**: Long-tail first → build topical authority → target competitive head terms
- **Quick wins checklist**: Fix technical issues, optimize existing pages (easier than creating new content), internal linking, refreshing outdated content
- **Realistic milestone framework**: Month 1–2 (indexing + crawling), Month 3–4 (initial rankings), Month 5–6 (traffic growth), Month 6–12 (competitive positions)
