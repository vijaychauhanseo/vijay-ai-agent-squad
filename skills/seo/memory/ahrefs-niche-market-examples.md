## Core Concept
7 real companies that built dominant market positions by going extremely narrow and deep on a specific audience. The lesson: niche depth beats broad coverage. This directly validates @learnaiwithvijay's strategy of owning "AI education for non-technical professionals" rather than competing in broad AI coverage.

## Key Takeaways
- Niching down = less competition, higher relevance, stronger community
- Every niche has underserved content needs — find them and own them completely
- Niche audiences are more loyal and more likely to share/recommend
- Being #1 in a small niche is worth more than being #50 in a large one
- Ahrefs itself is example #7: they niched into "website owners who want SEO data" — now dominant

## The 7 Examples

| Company | Niche | Why It Works |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Lefty's | Left-handed people | Ignored by mainstream retailers |
| Nomad List | Digital nomads | Built community around shared lifestyle |
| The Modest Man | Short men's fashion | Underserved by standard fashion content |
| TomBoyX | LGBTQ+ inclusive clothing | Authentic community representation |
| Bee's Wrap | Sustainable food storage | Values-driven purchase decision |
| Divvies | Vegan + nut-allergy treats | Safety need + dietary preference combo |
| Ahrefs | Website owners needing SEO data | Technical product, specific professional need |
