## Core Concept
Ahrefs ran a real experiment buying black hat link building services to test whether they actually improve rankings. Result: they don't work and create risk of Google penalties. Data-driven proof to avoid these tactics.

## Key Takeaways
- Black hat link services (Fiverr gigs, "10,000 backlinks for $5") deliver zero ranking improvement
- Links from link farms are immediately devalued or ignored by Google's algorithms
- Many "links" delivered don't even stay live after 30 days
- Google is extremely good at identifying and ignoring low-quality, unnatural links
- Risk: if Google detects a pattern, manual penalty can tank your entire site
- The only links that work are editorial links — earned through content quality or outreach
- Money wasted on black hat links is better spent on one good guest post or digital PR piece

## Data/Stats (from Ahrefs experiment)
- Bought a popular black hat service and tracked results over 30+ days
- Rankings: no measurable improvement on target keywords
- Link quality: mostly foreign-language sites, link farms, spammy directories
- Link retention: significant % of links disappeared within weeks
- Word count: 2,991 words
