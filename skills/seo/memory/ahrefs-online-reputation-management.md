## Core Concept
Online Reputation Management (ORM) = controlling what people see when they search for you or your brand. The goal is to push positive, authoritative content to page 1 while suppressing or addressing negative content. Critical for any creator or brand building a public presence.

## Key Takeaways
- ORM = proactive + reactive: build positive content before crises, respond to negatives when they appear
- Google's SERP is your reputation — what ranks for your name IS your brand perception
- 3 main threats: negative reviews, bad press/articles, social media criticism
- You cannot delete negative content you don't own — you can only outrank it
- Proactive ORM: build strong profiles on LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube, Medium — these rank for your name
- Reactive ORM: respond professionally to negative reviews (shows future customers you care)
- Social listening = monitoring brand mentions across the web before they become crises

## Frameworks/Methods Covered

**ORM on Google:**
1. Claim and optimize all social profiles (LinkedIn, YouTube, Twitter) — these rank for brand name searches
2. Create content that ranks: blog posts, interviews, podcast appearances, press mentions
3. Build Wikipedia or Crunchbase profiles if you qualify
4. Respond to Google Reviews — both positive and negative, professionally

**ORM on Social Media:**
1. Set up Google Alerts for your brand name
2. Monitor brand mentions using tools like Mention, Brand24, or Ahrefs Alerts
3. Respond to all negative mentions quickly — silence = guilt in public perception
4. Address criticism factually, never emotionally
