## Core Concept
Ahrefs analyzed 2 million featured snippets to understand how they work, who gets them, and how to win them. Key finding: featured snippets significantly increase click-through rates and you don't need to rank #1 to get them.

## Key Takeaways
- Featured snippets appear in ~12.3% of all search queries
- Pages with featured snippets get significantly more clicks than #1 results without them
- You DON'T need to rank #1 — most featured snippets come from positions 2–5
- Most featured snippets come from pages already in the top 10
- Question-based queries (how, what, why, who) trigger featured snippets most often
- Paragraph snippets are most common, followed by lists and tables
- Featured snippets change frequently — winning them requires ongoing optimization
- Backlinks alone don't determine who gets the snippet — content format matters more

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **How to win featured snippets**:
  1. Find keywords with existing snippets (Ahrefs KE → SERP features filter)
  2. Check if you're already in top 10 for that keyword
  3. Format your content to match snippet type (paragraph = direct answer, list = numbered steps, table = comparison data)
  4. Use the exact question as an H2 heading, answer it directly in the first paragraph below
- **Snippet trigger words**: "how to", "what is", "best", "how much", "who", "why", "recipe"

## Data/Stats (from 2M snippet study)
- 12.3% of queries have featured snippets
- Featured snippet pages average 8x more traffic than non-snippet top-10 pages (estimated)
- 99.58% of featured snippet URLs are already indexed in Google's top 10
- Single page can rank for multiple featured snippets simultaneously
- Ahrefs.com wins more featured snippets than any other domain in their dataset
