## Core Concept
Ahrefs study of 863K SERPs revealing how Google's AI Overviews select citation sources. The headline: only 38% of AI Overview citations come from top 10 ranking pages — down massively from 76% a year ago. This means ranking #1 no longer guarantees AI Overview inclusion, and non-ranking sources (especially YouTube) are being cited heavily.

## Key Takeaways
- **38%** of AI Overview citations come from top 10 organic results (down from **76%** a year ago)
- This is a massive shift — Google AI Overviews are increasingly pulling from outside the top 10
- **62%** of citations come from pages NOT in the top 10 — domain authority and topical relevance matter more than ranking position
- **18% of non-ranking citations come from YouTube** — video content is a significant AI Overview source
- Ranking well helps but is no longer sufficient for AI Overview inclusion
- AI Overviews favor authoritative, comprehensive sources — even if they rank on page 2 or 3
- Study based on 863,000 SERPs — statistically significant, not anecdotal

## Data/Stats
- Dataset: 863,000 SERPs analyzed
- 38% of AI Overview citations = top 10 pages (2026)
- 76% of AI Overview citations = top 10 pages (2025) — dropped by half in one year
- 18% of non-ranking citations = YouTube videos
- Word count: 2,709 words
- Published: March 2026 (very recent — reflects current AI Overview behavior)
