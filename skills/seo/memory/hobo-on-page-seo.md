## Core Concept
The Google Content Warehouse leak reveals that on-page SEO is now about a layered set of quantitative signals — from URL history and content effort scores to click validation and E-E-A-T overlays — replacing the old keyword-density model entirely.

## Key Takeaways
- Google's internal systems (Mustang + NavBoost) work together: Mustang scores the document, NavBoost validates it with clicks
- URL history matters: Google tracks host age, URL changes, and content versioning — old stable URLs rank easier
- "Content Effort" is a real leaked signal — Google quantifies how much work went into a page (depth, structure, richness)
- Chrome user data feeds directly into quality scoring even for non-click signals
- Site authority creates a "halo effect" — a high-authority domain lifts individual pages even before they have click data
- Editorial whitelists exist for sensitive topics (health, finance, news) — E-E-A-T is mandatory in these areas
- The "Disconnected Entity Hypothesis": if your author/brand isn't a known entity, you start with a trust deficit
- E-E-A-T is a guiding principle that overlays the entire ranking system — not just a checklist item

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **Mustang scoring**: Document-level quality assessment including content effort, semantic signals, and click data
- **URL as Signal**: Host age, URL structure, content version history all contribute to ranking
- **Content Effort Signal**: Quantitative measure of depth, richness, and investment in a page
- **User Validation Loop**: Clicks + Chrome data confirm or deny Mustang's initial quality score
- **Site Authority Halo**: How domain-level signals propagate down to individual pages
- **E-E-A-T Overlays**: When and how Google applies editorial quality filters on top of algorithmic scoring
