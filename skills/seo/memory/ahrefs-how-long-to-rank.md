## Core Concept
Ahrefs' data study (updated 2025) on how long it actually takes pages to reach the top 10 on Google. The data is sobering: most new pages never reach the top 10 in the first year. But the pages that do rank share predictable characteristics.

## Key Takeaways
- Most top-ranking pages are 2+ years old — Google rewards pages that have stood the test of time
- New pages that reach top 10 quickly are outliers, not the norm
- Pages with strong backlink profiles rank faster than those without
- Domain authority matters: a new page on an established domain ranks faster than the same page on a new domain
- Updating existing content often faster ROI than publishing new content — Google already knows the URL
- Patience is a competitive advantage — most marketers give up before SEO compounds

## Data/Stats (2025 study)
- Word count: 1,651 words (concise data study)
- Top-ranking pages are significantly older in 2025 vs 2017 data — competition has intensified
- Median age of top-ranking pages: 2-3+ years
- New pages reaching top 10 within 1 year: a small minority
- Pages on established, authoritative domains rank considerably faster

## Frameworks/Methods Covered

**How to rank faster:**
1. Publish on an established domain with existing authority
2. Build backlinks immediately after publishing (first 30 days matter most)
3. Target lower-competition keywords for new pages
4. Update existing ranking content instead of creating new pages for the same topic
5. Focus on search intent match — algorithmically correct pages skip the sandbox faster

**The ranking timeline reality:**
- Month 1-3: Indexation, initial crawl, almost no ranking
- Month 3-6: Starts appearing for long-tail variations, first positioning data in GSC
- Month 6-12: Rankings stabilize, movement accelerates with links
- Month 12+: Compound growth begins, existing authority helps new pages rank faster
